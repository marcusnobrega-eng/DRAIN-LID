Example 01 — Infiltration into Sandy Soil (Dirichlet head)¶

Goal¶

Demonstrate a sharp wetting front produced by constant pressure head infiltration into a dry sand column and compare against HYDRUS-1D.

How to run¶

Uncomment the line below in Mixed_Richards_Model_1D.m and run the script:

% load('Examples/Celia1990.mat');
load('Examples/Example1_Infiltration_Sand.mat');
% load('Examples/Example2_Clay_Loam_Soil.mat');
% ...

Scenario at-a-glance¶

  • Column depth: \(L = 1.0\) m; nodes: \(N_z = 41\) (uniform). :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

  • Time setup: total time \(T_{\max}=3600\) s (1 h); initial \(\Delta t=1\) s; save every 60 s.

  • Soil (USDA sand): \(\theta_r=0.045\), \(\theta_s=0.430\), \(\alpha=14.5\,\text{m}^{-1}\), \(n=2.68\), \(K_s=8.25\times10^{-5}\,\text{m s}^{-1}\), \(S_s=10^{-5}\,\text{m}^{-1}\).

  • Initial condition: hydrostatic (dry near surface).

  • Boundary conditions: - Top: Dirichlet (fixed head) \(h_\text{top}=0\) m. - Bottom: free drainage, \(\partial h/\partial z = 0\).

  • No drainage structures or internal sinks are active.

What to expect¶

  • A sharp wetting front initiates at the surface and propagates downward rapidly; no surface ponding is expected for this sandy profile. :

  • Agreement with HYDRUS-1D is strong for both \(h(z,t)\) and \(\theta(z,t)\) (low RMSE), with small deviations mainly at very early/late times near the surface.

  • The manual includes comparison plots (RMSE and profiles) for this case.

Output¶

Running this example will save time series and vertical profiles (head, water content, flux) and summary figures into the scenario’s output folders (see Output and Diagnostics for file layout).
